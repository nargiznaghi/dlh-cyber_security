# Board Pitch: Enterprise Security Strategy Investment

## Current State
After a four-week security assessment, MedDefense operates on an unsegmented flat network with unmonitored systems and single-factor remote access[cite: 3]. Our security posture is functionally defenseless against modern cyber threats[cite: 3]. **The verdict:** We are running clinical operations on an exposed digital foundation where a single compromised account can halt hospital services[cite: 3].

## The Risk
If MedDefense takes no action, an attacker exploiting an unauthenticated remote connection can encrypt our core health databases with ransomware[cite: 3]. This single scenario results in an **Annualized Loss Expectancy (ALE) of $3.62 Million** in recovery costs, regulatory HIPAA fines, and operational downtime[cite: 3]. Across our top risks, doing nothing leaves $6.8 Million in annual financial exposure on the table[cite: 3].

## The Plan
We recommend deploying a targeted $76,000 defensive strategy well within the Board's $120,000 budget ceiling[cite: 3]. This plan prioritizes three core controls: Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on remote access, Network Segmentation to isolate clinical systems, and Offsite Immutable Cloud Backups[cite: 3]. Together, these three controls eliminate over 96% of our overall financial risk exposure within six months without disrupting clinical care workflows[cite: 3].

## The Return
This strategic investment delivers an extraordinary **86:1 Return on Investment (ROI)**[cite: 3]. For every $1 invested in this program, MedDefense avoids **$86.49 in expected financial losses**, protecting patient safety, operational continuity, and corporate reputation[cite: 3].
