## 2. Top 3 Priority Threat Actor Ranking

### 1. Ransomware Groups (Organized Crime)
* **Justification:** This actor category represents the absolute highest risk profile to MedDefense due to a combination of maximum likelihood and devastating operational impact. RaaS operators specifically target mid-size healthcare organizations because they recognize that clinical downtime directly endangers patient lives, forcing rapid ransom payouts. Our posture assessment proves that MedDefense possesses the exact technical vulnerabilities—unpatched firewalls, lack of internal network segmentation, missing MFA on administrative portals, and network-exposed backups—that these groups actively hunt. A compromise would result in a simultaneous data breach event and total operational paralysis, turning off medical systems and forcing ambulance diversions for weeks.

### 2. Insider (Malicious)
* **Justification:** While less frequent than negligent actions, a malicious insider holds verified, trusted access that bypasses almost all external perimeter security devices entirely. The potential impact is catastrophic because a disgruntled technical employee can intentionally disable system backups, construct hidden backdoors, and delete production databases within minutes. Given MedDefense's lack of centralized logging (SIEM/IDS) and poor identity lifecycle management—where contractor credentials stay active for 47 days past termination—a malicious insider can operate with total anonymity. This allows them to systematically sabotage hospital systems or leak sensitive PHI for financial blackmail without triggering any real-time alerts.
