# Board Briefing: Urgent Security Posture Alignment

### Executive Address

MedDefense currently operates under a severe security deficit, relying on basic outer defenses that are easily bypassed by modern cyber threats. Once an attacker gets past our perimeter, there are no internal boundaries or monitoring systems to stop them from freezing our entire network.

Our single most dangerous gap is a completely flat network combined with zero security monitoring. This means a simple phishing email on an administrative laptop can instantly spread, letting hackers encrypt life-critical medical equipment and patient databases without triggering a single alert.

To address this, we must execute three immediate priority actions:

1. **Isolate Medical Devices and Servers:** Segment our network to confine any compromise to its starting point.  
   * *Cost:* $40,000 | *Timeline:* 3 months.
2. **Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Force secondary login verification for all remote access and clinical applications.  
   * *Cost:* $20,000 | *Timeline:* 1 month.
3. **Outsource 24/7 Security Monitoring:** Partner with a managed security service provider to detect and isolate threats immediately.  
   * *Cost:* $21,000 | *Timeline:* 1 month.

### The Business Case

Approving this $120,000 security budget is a critical operational investment, not an administrative expense. A single hospital ransomware breach easily costs over $1.5 million in recovery fees, legal penalties, and lost patient revenue. Spending $120,000 now prevents a multimillion-dollar disaster later.

If we take no action today, we accept the high probability of a catastrophic system outage that will directly compromise patient safety, invite federal fines, and permanently damage our community trust.
