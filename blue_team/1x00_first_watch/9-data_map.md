# Data Lifecycle Map and Risk Assessment

This document maps the flow, storage, and processing states of MedDefense's data assets against professional classification levels, pinpointing critical protection failures across data at rest, in transit, and in use.

## 2. Data Risk Summary

MedDefense's most severe and immediate data protection failure resides within its **Restricted Patient Medical Records (EHR)** during their **In Transit** and **In Use** states. While the data category possesses the highest classification level—carrying immense clinical, legal, and financial liability—its operational deployment lacks fundamental defensive safeguards. Mechanically, patient health records move across an entirely flat, unsegmented network topology in unencrypted cleartext streams, rendering them immediately vulnerable to packet sniffing or lateral extraction by any compromised node on the network. Socially and procedurally, this failure is amplified in the "In Use" state by institutional policies that forbid logging out at nursing terminals, leaving live clinical systems permanently unlocked and exposed to unauthorized physical viewing, theft, or manipulation by malicious third parties or passersby.
